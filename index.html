ABSTRACT

  DNA can be aggregated with complementarity, and liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) and gelation of DNA molecules have been observed depending on the concentration. If this aggregation can be controlled, various applications such as reaction field formation and specific trapping and release of molecules, substances, and cells are possible. Therefore, the achievement of gelation depended on linker DNA has been reported, but this kind of controlling gelation are limited to DNA structures with three or more sticky ends, such as Y-motifs. Furthermore, the incorporation of important sequences would increase the size of the structure and correspondingly decrease the density of the DNA, making the gel structure unstable. Thus, we propose a new method to control DNA gel formation.
  We focused on RCA, which has an accomplishment in both gelation and Origami structures, and designed a device that can release long-stranded DNA in an input-dependent manner to form gels. We named this device "DNA Mochi Robot".
  We, Tokyo Alliance in BIO-MOD, worked to design and assay this device.
